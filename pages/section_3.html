Mobile First

Aram Bartholl, Open, 2019

The mobile first approach in web design is a strategy that prioritizes the design and development of a website for mobile devices before creating a desktop version. This approach is driven by the increasing number of users who access the internet through their mobile devices. Over the past decade, the number of people using mobile devices to browse the internet has skyrocketed. According to Statista, the number of global mobile users is expected to reach 4.68 billion by 2021. With such a large percentage of internet users relying on their mobile devices, it’s crucial for web designers to create mobile-friendly websites that provide an optimal user experience. One advantage of the mobile first approach is the ability to utilize the hardware sensors in a mobile phone to create innovative web interfaces and user experiences. For example, mobile phones have accelerometers, which can be used to create interactive web experiences that respond to the movement of the device. Additionally, touch capabilities and high-performance cameras in mobile devices allow for new and creative ways of interaction on the web. Another benefit of the mobile first approach is the ability to control a desktop environment with a mobile interface. The desktop may not have an accelerometer, but the data can be transmitted from a mobile device to the computer, allowing for a unique interaction between the two. The touch capability of mobile devices also opens up new opportunities for web designers. For example, designers can use touch gestures to navigate through a website, adding an extra layer of interactivity and engagement for the user. The high-performance cameras in mobile devices also provide a new way of interacting with web content. For instance, augmented reality applications can be created, which allow users to interact with digital objects in the real world through their mobile device.

The HTC Vive Flow not only innovates in that you look less like a dweeb when wearing the thing, compared to most products in this branch, they have also implemented a phone remote: you connect your phone to the headset via bluetooth and it is then tracked with the onboard cameras for it to act as a remote for the headset.

For me this is a very interesting product because it gives the phone a new layer of functionality. It’s a really obvious solution, the technology to make it happen is there and it makes sense to experiment with it. It makes me wander how the decision was made at a high level in a tech corporation such as HTC, in the end you are selling one less thing to the customer so you make less money. In a world where profit tramps usability I think it’s a very bold and respectable choice form the Taiwanese giant. On another hand, HTC’s main product are indeed smartphones, so you’re probably likely to already have one of their smartphones if you’re buying their VR headset.

This product / experiment makes me raise several questions: How would using the phone compare to an ergonomic, purposely designed remote? Is a remote necessary for those interested only in watching VR media and not engaging in interactive media? Can the touchscreen and the interactions it brings be better than a remote (think gestures, multi-touch or swiping across a digital claypot as in link #2)?

"From its earliest days, digital design practice has been focused on creating interfaces for computers. Screen-based interactions are now mainly happening through smartphones and mobile-first experiences have become the norm."

In conclusion, the mobile first approach in web design is crucial in today’s world, where the majority of internet users access the web through their mobile devices. By prioritizing the design and development of a mobile-friendly website, designers can take advantage of the hardware sensors, touch capabilities, and high-performance cameras in mobile devices to create innovative and interactive user experiences. Mobile devices will play a significant role in shaping the future of web design, particularly in regards to WebXR and VR. With the increasing power and capabilities of mobile devices, users are now able to experience virtual and augmented reality experiences on-the-go. This opens up new possibilities for web designers to create immersive and interactive experiences for users. WebXR, a web-based platform for virtual and augmented reality, allows designers to create and distribute XR experiences to a wide audience without the need for specialized hardware or software. With the increasing number of mobile devices that are equipped with the necessary sensors and processing power, users will be able to access these experiences anywhere and at any time. VR, in particular, has the potential to completely revolutionize the way we interact with the web. With VR, users can be fully immersed in a virtual world, which opens up new opportunities for designers to create unique and engaging experiences. For example, designers can create virtual storefronts that allow users to shop and interact with products in a completely immersive environment. In summary, mobile devices will continue to play a crucial role in shaping the future of web design, particularly in regards to WebXR and VR. As mobile devices become more powerful and capable, web designers will have new tools and opportunities to create innovative and immersive experiences for users.
